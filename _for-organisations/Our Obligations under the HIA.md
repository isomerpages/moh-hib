<h2>What are my obligations</h2>
<h3>All healthcare providers are required to contribute selected health information to NEHR</h3>
<p>• Private and Public healthcare providers licensed under the Healthcare
Services Act (HCSA).</p>
<p>• Retail pharmacy licensees licensed under the Health Products Act (HPA).</p>
<p>• Public agencies who provide healthcare services e.g., Singapore Armed
Forces (SAF) clinics under the Ministry of Defence (MINDEF) and Singapore
Civil Defence Force (SCDF) under the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA).</p>
<h3>Type of health information to be contributed</h3>
<p>• List of data to contribute</p>
<p>• Only the health information of Singaporeans, Permanent Residents and
long-term residents with Foreign Identification Number (FIN).</p>
<p>• Providers are only responsible for contributing the data that they generate</p>
<p>• Note: outside of these data types, other types of health information
e.g., diabetic foot/ eye screening/ACP can still be contributed to NEHR
through other legal basis (e.g. seeking patient consent, rely on other
legislation such as PDPA or PSGA).</p>
<h3>List of users who can access NEHR</h3>
<p>• Private and Public healthcare providers licensed under the HCSA.</p>
<p>• Retail pharmacy licensees licensed under the HPA.</p>
<p>• Public agencies who provide healthcare services e.g., SAF clinics and
SCDF paramedics.</p>
<p>• Community health providers who are not licensed under HCSA and involved
in care delivery.</p>
<p>• MOH approval is required for entities’ access to NEHR.</p>
<h3>Access permitted only for provision of healthcare services and community health services</h3>
<p>• Provide definition of healthcare services</p>
<p>• Provide definition of community health services</p>
<p>• Prohibited purposes:</p>
<p>o Any employment purpose (except for statutory medical examinations prescribed
under the HIA); or</p>
<p>o Assessment of insurability.</p>
<p>• Link to NEHR guidelines for more information</p>
<h3>Providing care for patients who restrict access to their NEHR</h3>
<p>• Access restriction apply only to users who are accessing the NEHR for
the purpose of patient care. However, it may be overridden during medical
emergencies.</p>
<p>• Sharing of opted out NEHR information will be allowed under the following
circumstances:</p>
<p>o Where required or permitted by other written law (e.g. the PSGA or CPC).</p>
<p>o Where requested on public health grounds and approved for sharing by
the Minister for Health or his delegated authorised officer.</p>
<p>• Explain how to facilitate access restrictions for patients who wish
to do so.</p>
<h3>Other purposes for which the use and access to NEHR are allowed</h3>
<p>• Access to NEHR records not allowed for any employment purpose (except
for statutory medical examinations prescribed under the HIA); or assessment
of insurability.</p>
<p>o List of statutory medical examinations prescribed under the HIA</p>
<p>• Identifiable health information may be extracted from NEHR for public
health purposes, and anonymised or aggregated health information may also
be extracted from NEHR for public interest purposes. The requestor needs
to apply to MOH. Include the list of obligations that the requestor must
fulfil.</p>

<h3>Requirements between the discloser and recipient must be specified in the data sharing agreement</h3>
<p>• A valid Data Sharing Agreement must be signed between the discloser
and recipient</p>
<p>• Key requirements between the Discloser (A) and Recipient (B) include:
particulars of A and B, types of health information, etc</p>
<p>• MOH has flexibility to prescribe new requirements for the sharing agreement.</p>
<h3>Which computer systems under a healthcare provider’s purview are affected?</h3>
<p>• Computer systems that are interconnected with NEHR or processes health
information will need to meet HIB cyber and data security requirements,
e.g.,</p>
<p>o GP’s computer that contributes information to the NEHR</p>
<p>o Nursing home’s computer system that has health information</p>
<p>o An acute hospital’s HR or staff admin/leave system</p>
