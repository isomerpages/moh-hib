<h2><em>All healthcare providers are required to contribute selected health information to NEHR</em></h2>
<p><em>• Private and Public healthcare providers licensed under the Healthcare Services Act (HCSA).</em>
</p>
<p><em>• Retail pharmacy licensees licensed under the Health Products Act (HPA).</em>
</p>
<p><em>• Public agencies who provide healthcare services e.g., Singapore Armed Forces (SAF) clinics under the Ministry of Defence (MINDEF) and Singapore Civil Defence Force (SCDF) under the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA).</em>
</p>
<h2><em>Type of health information to be contributed</em></h2>

</p>
<p><em>Key Health Information with value for continuity of care will be contributed</em>
</p>
<p><em>• Only the health information of Singaporeans, Permanent Residents and long-term residents with Foreign Identification Number (FIN).</em>
</p>
<p><em>• Providers are only responsible for contributing the data that they generate.</em>
</p>
<h2><em>List of users who can access NEHR</em></h2>
<p><em>• Private and Public healthcare providers licensed under the HCSA.</em>
</p>
<p><em>• Retail pharmacy licensees licensed under the HPA.</em>
</p>
<p><em>• Public agencies who provide healthcare services e.g., SAF clinics and SCDF paramedics.</em>
</p>
<p><em>• Community health providers who are not licensed under HCSA and involved in care delivery.</em>
</p>
<p><em>• MOH approval is required for entities’ access to NEHR.</em>
</p>

<h2><em>Providing care for patients who restrict access to their NEHR</em></h2>
<p><em>Access restriction applies only to users who are accessing the NEHR for the purpose of patient care. However, it may be overridden during medical emergencies.</em>
</p>
<p><em>Sharing of opted out NEHR information will be allowed under the following circumstances:</em>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><em>Where required or permitted by other written law (e.g. the PSGA or CPC).</em>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><em>Where requested on public health grounds and approved for sharing by the Minister for Health or his delegated authorised officer.</em>
</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h2><em>Requirements between the discloser and recipient must be specified in the data sharing agreement</em></h2>
<p><em>• A valid Data Sharing Agreement must be signed between the discloser and recipient</em>
</p>
<p><em>• Key requirements between the Discloser (A) and Recipient (B) include: particulars of A and B, types of health information, etc</em>
</p>
<p><em>• MOH has flexibility to prescribe new requirements for the sharing agreement.</em>
</p>
<h2><em>Which computer systems under a healthcare provider’s purview are affected?</em></h2>
<p><em>Computer systems that are interconnected with NEHR or processes health information will need to meet HIB cyber and data security requirements.</em>
</p>
<p><em>For example:</em>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><em>GP’s computer that contributes information to the NEHR</em>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><em>Nursing home’s computer system that has health information</em>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><em>An acute hospital’s HR or staff admin/leave system</em>
</p>
</li>
</ul>
